Phenomenon.
Test the Equation.

Most physics teaching shows you the equation and asks you to trust it. These simulations show you the phenomenon and let you test whether the equation actually captures what's happening. Some of what you'll see has never been tested experimentally. Other parts contradict what's in the textbooks. All of it is designed to make you think.

These simulations are not proofs. They are visual demonstrations of alternative hypotheses that make testable predictions. Some predictions align with current observations; others diverge from accepted theory. Where they diverge, specific experiments are proposed. The goal is not to convince you that the standard model is wrong, but to show you that alternative explanations exist — and that many of them have never been experimentally ruled out.

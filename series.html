It's All About the Resources

From India's Aravalli range to Venezuela's oil fields and Greenland's minerals — the same ancient logic repeating at civilisational scale. Every convenience requires materials and energy. Why Elon Musk Is Dead Wrong About Solar: The Math That Destroys the Dream

The caveman invented fire because sunlight was too dilute to cook meat, stay warm, or see in the dark. Sunlight was free, it was everywhere, and it was not enough. So he built something denser. Hundreds of thousands of years later, the world's most prominent technologist is championing what even the caveman rejected. This article does the one calculation the energy debate refuses to do — energy density — and shows why that rejection was correct then and remains correct now.

We Are Inside
a Bubble,
Tumbling Through Space

Held by Something Nobody Understands

Gravity holds you to this planet, the planet to the Sun, the Sun in the galaxy. We have written beautiful equations about it. We have never once explained what it actually is.

Physical Universe Series Gravity Wonder
scroll

Look up. Every star you can see is there because of the same mysterious pulling that keeps you on this planet. We have written beautiful equations about it. We have built cathedrals of mathematics around it. We have never once explained what it actually is.

Newton spent nearly ten years on the problem and came away with two realisations. First, that he could accurately predict the motion of the Sun, the Moon, the Earth, and the other five planets then known. Second, that he could do this without understanding what actually makes them move. In his own words: "Thus far I have explained the phenomena of the heavens and of our sea by the force of gravity, but I have not yet assigned a cause to gravity." He described the pulling with extraordinary precision and told us plainly he had not explained it. That was 1687. Nobody has explained it since.

Einstein went further — a hypothetical four-dimensional canvas of space and time, matter bending it in exactly Newton's pattern. He was not wrong. But he was also not asking the same question the child asks. Newton and Einstein gave us the pattern. Neither gave us the thing that makes the pattern.

This article is not about the pattern. It is about the thing.

· · ·

You Have Never Felt Any of This

The Earth is spinning at 1,600 kilometres per hour at the equator — almost twice a commercial plane's cruising speed. Fast enough that if the mysterious pulling suddenly stopped, you would fly off sideways into space. The Earth is also hurtling around a star at 30 kilometres per second — 100 times the speed of sound. The Sun moves through the galaxy at 220 kilometres per second. The fastest man-made object ever built, the Parker Solar Probe, reaches 500 times the speed of sound — and this is still just 0.06% of the speed of light.

You have never felt any of this motion. Not once. Not for a heartbeat. You sit still, reading these words, and the reason you feel nothing — the reason you have not drifted off the planet — is something constant and total and completely unexplained, which we have given the very ordinary name of gravity.

The Bubble You Are Inside

There is a thin shell of air around this rock — so thin that if you shrank the Earth to the size of a school globe, the atmosphere keeping you alive would be thinner than a coat of varnish on the surface. Beyond that varnish: hard vacuum, temperatures near absolute zero, radiation that would strip you bare. The only reason that shell of air is there, the only reason it has stayed for four and a half billion years without drifting off into space, is this same mysterious pulling. It will not let the air go. It will not let anything go. And everything rotates with the Earth — the sky above and the moons beyond, all in the same direction as the rock rotates.

P1 — Radial directionality

Gravity pulls everything toward the centre of mass — always inward, along the line connecting the two bodies, without exception.

P2 — Continuous field

Gravity acts everywhere, at every moment, without interruption. There is no location in the observable universe where it is absent.

Not in the way you might politely say you don't know something while knowing most of it. We genuinely, completely, have no idea what is happening when one thing pulls toward another across empty space. We have measured it. We have described the pattern. We have used those descriptions to land spacecraft on comets and predict where Neptune would be before any telescope was pointed at it. But a description of a mysterious happening is not the same as understanding the mysterious happening.

Every star you have ever seen is there because of it. Every mountain, every ocean, the air you are breathing, the bones in your body — all shaped by a mysterious pulling that nobody has ever traced to its source.

· · ·

Is It Even Pulling?

We know gravity is not like glue — our feet do not stick to the ground. Not like static electricity — it does not care how much charge you carry. Not like a magnet. And here is the remarkable thing: it does not care if you are a feather or a gigantic piece of asteroid. A hammer and a feather, dropped together on the Moon — as an Apollo astronaut did in 1971 — land at exactly the same moment. The pulling grabs things of completely different sizes, materials, temperatures, and pulls each one identically.

The Feather and the Hammer

Gravity treats everything the same. Everything moves at the same rate: down, toward the centre of this rock. Newton would say gravity adjusts its force to match each object's mass. Einstein says both objects follow the same curved slope in spacetime. But consider a river: if you are on a boat, you move at the same speed as the river — not faster or slower. A log moves the same. A feather too. If the river were invisible, you would call it magic. The equivalence of all objects in gravity's grip is one of its deepest properties — confirmed to one part in a hundred trillion.

P8 — Equivalence principle

Gravity accelerates all objects identically regardless of mass, composition, or internal state. Confirmed to 1 part in 10¹⁵ — the most precisely tested relationship in physics.

And then the child asks the question that stops the whole room: wait — is it even pulling? Or is something pushing?

If something invisible were pressing you toward the Earth from every direction above — from all sides except below — you would feel exactly the same thing as being pulled downward. You could not tell the difference from inside. The result looks identical. So when we say gravity pulls, are we describing what is actually happening, or just describing which way things move? Nobody has ruled out the pushing alternative. Nobody has even seriously tried.

The scientists have an answer ready. There is, they say, a tiny particle — the graviton — that carries the pulling between things. Except nobody has found it. Not in eighty years of searching. Not a trace. It is the most searched-for thing in the history of science and it is simply not there. So the honest answer to the child is: we do not know. We have a confident name for the thing we have never found. The child is right to keep asking. In fact the child may be the most qualified person in the room.

What the Pulling Actually Does

The Dead Sea

Walk into the Dead Sea and you cannot sink. You float on your back, effortlessly, reading a newspaper in the middle of a salty lake. Nothing about the pulling toward the Earth has changed. So what changed? Gravity has created a density gradient field. Each point in the fluid is an equilibrium point — lower density than the medium and you rise, higher density and you sink. Heat a gas so it expands and its density drops, and it rises. The pulling does not act on surfaces. It acts on every atom individually and simultaneously. You cannot fool it by rearranging the shape of something.

P7 — Volume phenomenon

Gravity responds to the total matter in a volume, acting on every atom individually. Unlike pressure — which acts on surfaces — gravity acts through the full depth of any object simultaneously.

P11 — Apparent inertia as density contrast

The apparent weight of an object in a medium depends on the density contrast between the object and the medium — Archimedes' buoyancy operating at every scale from a dead sea to a galaxy.

You Cannot Fool the Scale

You are at a market buying oranges. You try to trick the scale — stack them differently, put a metal plate under them. The number does not move. The pulling reaches straight through everything and takes hold of every orange with the same grip regardless of arrangement. You cannot change what something weighs by rearranging it. Always. Without exception.

Every other force in nature can be redirected or blocked. Light with your hand. Magnetism inside a coil. Electricity through a wire. Radiation behind lead. Nobody has ever reduced gravity by the smallest measurable amount — with any material, any geometry, any thickness, anywhere, ever.

P4 — Non-shieldable

No material, geometry, or physical arrangement has ever blocked, reduced, or redirected gravitational attraction. Unlike every other known force, gravity cannot be screened.

You Are 2 Centimetres Taller in the Morning

All night, lying flat, the squeezing along your spine is relieved and the pads between your vertebrae quietly expand. Every morning you are briefly taller. Every day the squeezing resumes. Your heart is one of the strongest muscles you have, relative to its size — it has to be, because it is pushing blood upward against the pull every second of your life. Lower back pain affects more than 600 million people on Earth right now. Your entire skeleton is a load-bearing architecture for a pulling that started the moment you were born and will not stop until you die. Astronauts who float free of the pull for six months return with bones that have begun dissolving the structures they no longer need. The body is sensible. Why maintain the scaffolding if the pulling has gone away?

P10 — Scales linearly with mass

In the tested regime, doubling the mass roughly doubles the gravitational effect. The force is proportional to the product of the masses involved.

Saturn's Rings and Instant Discs

Saturn's rings span 270,000 kilometres across and in places are barely 10 metres deep — if you scaled them to the size of a sheet of paper, the paper would be 6 kilometres wide. They are not gradually settling into this shape. They already are this shape, have been for billions of years, will be for billions more. Every disc in the universe — Saturn's rings, the planets all orbiting in nearly the same flat plane, the Milky Way itself — has this geometry. It appears immediately, before anything has had time to settle. A cloud of spinning matter does not gradually become a disc. It snaps into one, almost at once. Nobody knows why discs.

P22 — Immediate disc formation

Rotating matter under gravity organises into flat discs far faster than gradual settling accounts for — at planetary ring scale, solar system scale, and galactic scale. The mechanism is not established.

Venus and the Air You Are Breathing

Venus and Earth started from the same cloud of material — same size, same distance from the Sun, same time. Venus should be Earth's twin. Instead it is a furnace: hotter than a kitchen oven at full blast, times four hundred. The air on its surface would crush you flat before the heat got you. Two almost identical beginnings. Two completely opposite outcomes. The difference came down to how strongly each planet pulled on its own air — which gases got held, which drifted off into space — and whether the planet stayed warm enough to generate a magnetic shield. You are breathing because this planet pulled just hard enough, for just long enough. Venus did not.

P5 — Atmospheric retention

Gravity determines which gases a planet holds and which escape to space. The composition of an atmosphere — and whether life is possible — is a direct function of gravitational field strength.

The Bigger the Ball, the More Magnetic

Everything that gets pulled together into a big spinning lump becomes magnetic. The Sun's magnetic reach extends past all the planets. Jupiter's shield is twenty times stronger than Earth's. Mars has almost none and long ago lost most of its air. The pattern holds across every body ever measured: more mass, more spin, more magnetism. Now hold a fridge magnet and make it twice as big. Does it become twenty times more magnetic? No. Something fundamentally different happens when the pulling squeezes things at planetary scale. The pulling and the magnetism seem to know about each other. Nobody has described the conversation between them — and that conversation is what keeps the air around this planet right now.

P23 — Rotation-magnetic dipole correlation

Gravitational self-compression of rotating matter generates magnetic fields whose strength scales with mass and rotation in ways that no current theory predicts from first principles.

Jupiter's Red Spot Has Been Spinning for 400 Years

Jupiter has a storm the size of the Earth that has been spinning continuously for at least four hundred years. On Earth, storms dissipate in days. The banded atmosphere of Jupiter — alternating stripes each spanning tens of thousands of kilometres — holds its structure with a rigidity terrestrial weather never shows. The same pattern appears at Saturn, Uranus, Neptune. Atmospheric banding and persistent storm systems appear wherever a strong gravitational field meets a deep rotating atmosphere. The mechanism connecting gravity's geometry to atmospheric structure has never been described.

P20 — Atmospheric banding

Strong gravitational fields organise deep rotating atmospheres into stable banded structures persisting on timescales of centuries. The mechanism is not established.

The Galactic Rotation Problem

Stars at the edge of a galaxy should be moving slowly — just as the outer planets move slower than the inner ones, because the Sun's grip weakens with distance. They are not. Stars at the outer edge of every galaxy we have measured orbit at nearly the same speed as those near the centre. The rotation curve stays flat when it should fall off. Every galaxy does this. The current explanation: invisible matter we have never detected, providing the missing gravitational grip. Fifty years of searching across every proposed particle type. Not one found.

P3 — Flat galactic rotation curves

Stars at galactic edges orbit at nearly the same speed as those near the centre. No known distribution of visible matter accounts for this. Confirmed across thousands of galaxies.

Frame Dragging — Something Is Being Rotated

When a massive object rotates, it drags something with it. Not the air, not the rock — something more fundamental. An orbit that should be stable precesses in exactly the way you would expect if the rotating body were slowly twisting something in the space around it. This effect — confirmed by Gravity Probe B in 2011 — is called frame dragging. The Earth's rotation drags the entire gravitational field around with it. The Sun's rotation does the same across the whole solar system. What exactly is being dragged has no physical description. The equations describe the amount of dragging precisely. What is being dragged is unnamed.

P6 — Frame dragging

A rotating massive body drags the gravitational field around with it. The effect is measured and confirmed. The physical substance being dragged has no agreed physical description.

Newton — Private Letter, 1693

"That gravity should be innate, inherent, and essential to matter, so that one body may act upon another at a distance through a vacuum, without the mediation of anything else... is to me so great an absurdity, that I believe no man who has in philosophical matters a competent faculty of thinking can ever fall into it."

He used the equations anyway, because they worked. He told us plainly he had described the pulling without explaining it — and that explaining it was still waiting. It is still waiting.

The Squeeze Nobody Has Explained

In 1938, Hans Bethe figured out what the Sun is doing. Hydrogen, deep in the middle of it, is turning into helium, releasing an enormous amount of energy. That is what sunlight is. The moment Bethe published this, the obvious thought occurred to everyone: if we know how it works, we can build one. A second Sun, here on Earth. Energy forever, for everyone, for free. The project began immediately. That was 87 years ago. It is still going.

How much money have we spent trying? Add it all up — every experiment, every machine, every laboratory on every continent, every researcher's salary for seven decades — and the total is above a hundred billion dollars. You know how much the world spends on lingerie — lace and silk undergarments, the decorative kind — in a single year? About a hundred billion dollars. Every year. The amount spent across seventy years and every nation on the most important energy project in human history is what humanity spends on decorative underwear in twelve months. Something about that comparison should give us pause. Not about the underwear. About the Sun.

But a child would ask a simpler question first. How does the Sun do it? Not how hot is it — but how does it hold everything together long enough for the squeezing to happen at all? The answer is: the same mysterious pulling that is holding you to your chair right now. The entire Sun is being pulled toward itself from every direction, permanently. The middle is squeezed so tightly by all the weight above it that hydrogen has no choice but to merge. That is what makes the Sun work. Not the heat alone — the squeeze. The heat is a consequence of the squeeze.

We have spent a hundred billion dollars and seventy years trying to make a second Sun without ever seriously asking what is doing the squeezing — because we have never understood the pulling. We are heating things to ten times the temperature of the Sun's core to make up for a squeeze we cannot replicate, because the squeeze comes from the thing nobody has explained.

333,000 Times the Earth. Only 28 Times the Pull.

We say with great confidence that the Sun has 333,000 times as much stuff as the Earth. And yet if you could stand on the surface of the Sun you would feel pulled down only about 28 times more strongly than on Earth. Twenty-eight. Not 333,000. A child would immediately ask: where did all the rest of the pulling go? The answer — that the Sun is wider so you are further from its middle — sounds sensible until you ask: but what is the middle doing? What is actually travelling from all that stuff to reach you? Nobody knows. We have the ratio. We have the numbers. We have never found the thing that produces them. And by the way: how do we know the Sun has 333,000 times Earth's stuff? Nobody has been there. We calculated it — from the way Earth moves around the Sun, using the same mathematical rules we are trying to understand. The number comes from the pattern, confirmed by the pattern. A very good calculation. Not a measurement.

P9 — Fusion extends the gravitational radius of action

The Sun's gravitational field extends to a boundary far beyond what the inverse square law applied to its derived mass predicts. The mechanism connecting nuclear fusion to the reach of the gravitational field has never been described.

P21 — Gravitational constant is contested

G — the number at the heart of every gravitational calculation — disagrees with itself by 500 parts per million across precision experiments. It is the worst-measured fundamental constant in physics. Every celestial mass expressed in kilograms is downstream of this uncertainty.

Everything We Know It Does — All 23 Properties

The wonder above is not cherry-picked. Gravity has twenty-three confirmed observational properties — things directly measured, not inferred from theory. Here they are, stated plainly. The scoring of the two great theories against each one comes in the next article.

P1

Radial directionality. Always toward the centre of mass. Never sideways, never outward.

P2

Continuous field. Acts everywhere, at every moment, without interruption.

P3

Flat galactic rotation curves. Stars at galactic edges orbit at the same speed as those near the centre. Unexplained by visible matter.

P4

Non-shieldable. No material or arrangement has ever blocked, reduced, or redirected it.

P5

Atmospheric retention. Determines which gases a planet holds and whether life is possible.

P6

Frame dragging. A rotating body drags the gravitational field around with it. What is being dragged has no physical description.

P7

Volume phenomenon. Acts on every atom in a volume individually. Cannot be fooled by rearranging the shape of matter.

P8

Equivalence principle. Accelerates all objects identically regardless of mass or composition. Confirmed to 1 in 10¹⁵.

P9

Fusion extends the radius of action. The Sun's gravitational reach extends beyond what its derived mass alone predicts.

P10

Scales linearly with mass. Double the mass, roughly double the gravitational effect — in the tested regime.

P11

Apparent inertia as density contrast. Buoyancy is gravity recognising the density difference between an object and its medium.

P12

Six confirmed gravity–EM interactions. Gravitational lensing, redshift, time dilation, frame dragging, Shapiro delay, gravitational waves — all couple gravity and electromagnetism.

P13

Inverse square law. The force reduces to a quarter at double the distance — in the inner solar system regime where it has been tested.

P14

Propagates at the speed of light. Changes in the gravitational field travel outward at c, confirmed by the 2017 neutron star merger observation.

P15

Slows time. Clocks at lower gravitational potential run slower. Your head ages fractionally faster than your feet. GPS is corrected for this every day.

P16

Compresses matter beyond any laboratory pressure. Neutron star interiors reach densities no terrestrial experiment approaches. The physical state of matter under this compression is unknown.

P17

Creates the conditions for nuclear fusion. Every star fuses hydrogen because gravity compresses it past the ignition threshold. We have not replicated this mechanism.

P18

Drives the expansion of space. Dark energy acts as a gravitational effect at cosmological scale, accelerating universal expansion. Its physical nature is entirely unknown.

P19

Acts as container and compressor simultaneously. Gravity holds matter together while compressing it toward a central point — opposite effects from the same mechanism.

P20

Atmospheric banding. Produces stable banded structures in deep rotating atmospheres, persistent for centuries. Mechanism unknown.

P21

Gravitational constant is contested. G disagrees with itself by 500 ppm across precision experiments — the worst-measured fundamental constant in physics.

P22

Immediate disc formation. Rotating matter under gravity snaps into flat discs rapidly at every scale. Mechanism not established.

P23

Rotation-magnetic dipole correlation. More massive rotating bodies generate stronger magnetic fields in ways no current theory predicts from first principles.

Twenty-three confirmed observational properties. The next article in this series takes the two great theories of gravity — Newton's and Einstein's — and scores them against each one. Not to be harsh. To see the shape of what remains. Because the shape of what we do not know is the best clue we have to what we are actually looking for.

And the shape, when you see it, has the outline of a mechanism. Patient, specific, and completely available to a child's question.

We know the pattern of the pulling with extraordinary precision. We have never found what is underneath the pattern. Those are not the same knowledge — and only one of them will let us do what the Sun does.

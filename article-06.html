Physics Has Mastered
5% of Gravity

An Inventory of What We Do Not Know About the Force That Governs Everything

Gravity is the force physics is most confident about. It is also the force physics understands least. This article does the arithmetic.

Physical Universe Series Gravity Foundation of the Model
scroll

Ask a physicist what they know about gravity and they will tell you about Newton, Einstein, GPS, gravitational waves. Three hundred and fifty years of increasingly precise equations. Agreement with observation to parts per billion in some regimes. The confidence is total, and in the narrow domain where it applies, it is earned. But there is a question the confidence does not answer, and it is the first question any engineer asks before they build something: how much of the problem do you actually understand?

The honest answer, once you do the arithmetic, is: approximately 5%.

Not 5% of the equations. Not 5% of the predictions. 5% of the gravitational content of the Universe — the fraction on which the equations have been tested and found to work. The other 95% has been labelled "dark" and deferred. On that 95%, the equations either fail, or something else is happening that we have not described. And on the 5% where the equations do work, the force carrier has never been detected, the fundamental constant is the worst-measured in all of physics, the simplest property of the force has no physical explanation after 350 years, and the theory predicts its own breakdown at every black hole in the Universe.

This is not a gap at the frontier of knowledge. It is a gap at the foundation. And it has a cost that is no longer abstract.

First, the Arithmetic

The Universe's mass-energy content breaks down as follows, according to the best available cosmological measurements. This is not this series' accounting — it is the standard model of cosmology, confirmed by the Planck satellite and accepted by every major physics institution.

Mass-Energy Content of the Observable Universe

The gravity we have tested and confirmed operates on the rightmost sliver. The rest has been named, not explained.

Dark energy — 68% of everything — drives the accelerating expansion of the Universe. Its physical nature is entirely unknown. The cosmological constant used to describe it was introduced by Einstein, retracted as his "greatest blunder," and reintroduced after the 2011 Nobel Prize with no additional physical content — just a number fitted to the observation. Dark matter — 27% of total mass-energy, or approximately 85% of all matter — is the dominant gravitational actor in every galaxy we have observed. Its physical nature is entirely unknown. Every proposed particle candidate has been searched for and not found: WIMPs across decades of experiments at LUX, XENON, and PandaX; axions at ADMX; sterile neutrinos at multiple reactors. The searches have not failed because the detectors were inadequate. They have failed because nothing has been detected.

What remains is 5% — ordinary baryonic matter, the stuff of atoms, the only content on which Newton's and Einstein's equations have been tested. That is the domain of our equations. That is the extent of our gravitational knowledge, measured by what we have actually confirmed the equations describe correctly.

The Six Failures on the 5% We Do Claim to Understand

Setting aside the 95% entirely: even within the domain where the equations work, the understanding is not what the confidence suggests. Here, in order of increasing embarrassment, is what remains unresolved on the 5% physics calls its own.

i
The Constant Is Contested Circular

G — the gravitational constant — is the worst-measured fundamental constant in physics. It disagrees with itself by 500 parts per million across precision experiments. Every other constant is pinned to parts per billion or better. G is still arguing about its fifth significant figure after 200 years of measurement. More fundamentally: G was extracted from the Cavendish torsion balance by assuming Newton's inverse square law is correct, then used to confirm Newton's inverse square law. The validation is inside the assumption. Every mass in the universe expressed in kilograms — every solar mass, every planetary mass, every black hole mass — is downstream of this circularity. We have not measured the mass of the Sun. We have calculated it from an equation we used to define the constant we used to do the calculation.

ii
The Equivalence Principle Has No Explanation Unknown

Gravitational mass and inertial mass are equal. This has been confirmed to 1 part in 10¹⁵ — among the most precisely verified equalities in all of physics. Why they are equal has no answer. Newton stated in 1687 that he framed no hypotheses on the matter. Einstein in 1915 elevated the equality to a postulate — a thing assumed to be true because assuming it produces correct results, not because we understand why it is true. Three hundred and fifty years. The most precisely confirmed fact about gravity. No mechanism. The question "why does the thing that resists acceleration and the thing that responds to gravity happen to be identical?" has received, in the entire history of physics, one answer: they just are.

iii
Gravity Cannot Be Shielded No Framework Exists

Every other force in nature can be screened or shielded. Electromagnetism: a Faraday cage, first built in 1836, blocks electric fields completely. A schoolchild can make one from aluminium foil. The strong nuclear force: confined to the nucleus — nature shields it automatically beyond femtometre scales. The weak force: similarly confined. Gravity: nothing. No material, no geometry, no configuration of any kind produces any attenuation of gravitational force. Not "we haven't found the right material." We have no theoretical framework for what shielding would even mean. This is not a practical inconvenience. It is a conceptual signal. Every other force can be stood outside of. Gravity cannot. Every technology that "manages" gravity — every bridge, aircraft, rocket, and space station — works by fighting it. We have had 350 years and we cannot do with gravity what any first-year electrical engineering student learns to do with electromagnetism in a semester.

iv
The Force Carrier Has Never Been Detected Zero Detections

Every other fundamental force has a confirmed quantum carrier. The photon carries electromagnetism — detected, characterised, engineered into lasers and LEDs. The W and Z bosons carry the weak force — Nobel Prize 1984. The gluon carries the strong force — Nobel Prize 2004. The graviton — the hypothetical carrier of gravity — has never been detected. Not once. There is no confirmed quantum theory of gravity. Quantum field theory and general relativity are the two most successful physical theories ever constructed, and they are mathematically incompatible: combining them produces infinities that cannot be removed. String theory has been working on this for forty years. Loop quantum gravity likewise. Neither has produced a confirmed experimental prediction. Gravity is the only fundamental force without a quantum description, without a detected carrier, and without a path to either that the community agrees on.

v
Rotation Curves — 50 Years, No Answer Unknown

In the 1970s, Vera Rubin measured the rotation speeds of stars at the edges of galaxies. By Newton's and Einstein's equations, stars far from the galactic centre should orbit more slowly, just as outer planets orbit the Sun more slowly than inner ones. They do not. Stars at the galaxy's edge orbit at the same speed as stars near the centre — in every galaxy measured, across thousands of galaxies, consistently and universally. The discrepancy is not small. It implies that either our equations for gravity are wrong at galactic scales, or there is a large amount of invisible mass arranged specifically to make them look right. The second option — dark matter — has been the preferred explanation for fifty years. The particle has not been found. The first option — that the equations need revision — means acknowledging that we do not know what gravity is at the scales where most of the gravitational action in the Universe takes place.

vi
The Theory Predicts Its Own Failure Unresolved

General relativity predicts that at the centre of every black hole, density becomes infinite and spacetime curvature becomes infinite. A physical theory that produces infinities as its own output is not complete — it is telling you that it has been pushed beyond its domain of validity. This is not a contested interpretation. It is the standard reading of the mathematics. The standard response is that quantum gravity will resolve the singularity — that the infinities will be tamed once gravity is quantised. Quantum gravity does not exist. The theory that describes gravity at large scales breaks down at small scales. The theory that describes everything else at small scales cannot be extended to gravity. The resolution is named but not built, and the breakdown is not at some exotic frontier. It is at the centre of every black hole in the Universe — of which there are estimated to be 40 billion in our galaxy alone.

Failures at Every Scale — From Our Own Solar System to the Edge of the Observable Universe

The six failures above are drawn from the mainstream literature. They are acknowledged, individually, as open problems. But the honest picture is worse than any individual failure suggests — because the failures are not scattered randomly. They are present at every scale at which we have tried to apply the equations. And the response to each one has followed the same pattern: add an invisible term, keep the equations, move on.

Start in our own solar system, where the equations are supposedly most reliable. The inverse square law predicts that the Sun's gravitational dominance ends at approximately 5,900 AU — the distance at which the Sun's pull equals the background gravitational pull of the Milky Way, pulling in a different direction. By that model, nothing beyond 5,900 AU should be gravitationally bound to the Sun. Long-period comets return from the Oort Cloud — at distances of up to 100,000 AU. They are observably bound. Either the inverse square law is a local approximation that fails at large distances, or there is mass we cannot see extending the Sun's gravitational reach, or the galactic background pull has been calculated incorrectly. All three possibilities imply something is wrong with the model. None has been resolved.

The Question That Has Not Been Asked

Does the Sun's gravitational field at large distances resemble a galactic rotation curve — flat, rather than falling as 1/r²? We do not know. There is no luminous tracer matter at those distances to measure. The Oort Cloud has never been directly observed — it is inferred from the comets that occasionally fall inward. The inverse square law has been tested with high precision only to a few AU. We are applying it to 100,000 AU and then to billions of light-years — without intermediate verification, without falsification attempts, and without asking whether the same anomaly present in every galaxy we have measured might also be present in our own backyard.

Scale outward. In every galaxy measured — and astronomers have now measured thousands — orbital velocity does not fall at the edge as the equations predict. The discrepancy is not small: at the galactic boundary, stars orbit two to three times faster than Newton and Einstein jointly predict for the visible mass. The preferred explanation is dark matter: an invisible substance, five times more abundant than ordinary matter, arranged in a specific halo profile around every galaxy in exactly the right configuration to make the equations look correct. The particle responsible has been searched for across five decades and multiple experimental programmes — LUX, XENON1T, PandaX, CDMS, ADMX — without a single confirmed detection. Each non-detection is reported as a new constraint. The possibility that the equations themselves need revision is not pursued with equivalent resources.

Scale outward again. The accelerating expansion of the Universe contradicts the naive expectation from any gravity-only model — gravity pulls, so expansion should decelerate. The preferred explanation is dark energy: a pressure term opposing gravity, constituting 68% of the total mass-energy content of the Universe, physically unidentified, with a value that cannot be derived from any other known quantity. The cosmological constant encoding it differs from quantum field theory's prediction for vacuum energy by between 56 and 120 orders of magnitude — the largest discrepancy between a theoretical prediction and an observation in the history of physics. Dark energy is not an explanation. It is a name for the gap between the equations and the Universe.

And now the rate itself is disputed. Two independent methods of measuring how fast the Universe is expanding give answers that disagree by approximately 10% — well outside the margin of measurement error. The Planck satellite infers a Hubble constant of approximately 67 km/s/Mpc from the cosmic microwave background. Direct distance ladder measurements return approximately 73 km/s/Mpc. The discrepancy has been confirmed across multiple independent datasets and multiple independent teams. It is called the Hubble tension, and it means the standard cosmological model — the model that contains dark matter and dark energy — cannot reconcile its own measurements of the same Universe.

Dark matter is the name we gave to the failure of gravity at galactic scales. Dark energy is the name we gave to the failure of gravity at cosmological scales. The Hubble tension is the name we gave to the failure of the framework that contains both.

What makes this pattern striking is not the failures individually. It is the epistemic situation they produce together. The equations have been tested with extraordinary precision in one regime: the solar system, at distances from sub-millimetre to a few astronomical units, using matter we can see and weigh. We have extrapolated those equations to galactic scales, to cosmological scales, to the full observable Universe — and at every scale beyond our local tested regime, the equations require an additional invisible term to match observation. Dark matter at galactic scales. Dark energy at cosmological scales. A cosmological constant with no physical identity. A Hubble constant that disagrees with itself. Each term added, each discrepancy named, each non-detection logged as a constraint rather than a falsification.

The entire observational basis for those terms comes from light — photons that left their sources billions of years ago, carrying information about matter we will never visit, processes we cannot reproduce, and distances we infer from assumptions about standard candles that are themselves calibrated from the same models we are trying to test. We are reading shadows on a wall and inferring the laws that govern everything from the shapes of those shadows, while doing almost nothing to falsify those laws in the one place where we could run a controlled experiment: our own solar system, at distances between a few AU and the Oort Cloud, where the inverse square law has never been directly tested and where the same flat-rotation anomaly present in every galaxy we have measured may already be present — unnoticed because we have not looked for it.

The Connection to Fusion — Why This Is Not an Academic Argument

The standard solar model is the model of the Sun that every fusion programme on Earth is attempting to reproduce. It is built on gravitational physics that, by the scorecard above, accounts for 53.9% of gravity's confirmed observational properties. It assumes the inverse square law applies inside a stellar object whose interior has never been directly observed. It assumes pressure equilibrium between inward gravitational compression and outward radiation pressure in a regime where neither the gravity model nor the nuclear physics model has been independently verified at the relevant conditions.

ITER — $65 billion — is engineering toward a target defined by that model. The target has not been reached after seventy years of fusion research. The working assumption is that the engineering is insufficient. The alternative — that the target itself is specified from an incomplete physical account — has not been systematically tested. Seven confirmed observational properties of gravity that no existing theory addresses include the ones most directly relevant to stellar fusion: P9 (fusion extends the gravitational radius of action), P16 (the field sets the pressure and temperature envelope), and P17 (the field is simultaneously the compressor and the container). A physical description of those three properties alone would change the specification of the target. At $65 billion per attempt, the cost of the wrong specification is not abstract.

The six failures above are drawn from the mainstream literature and are widely acknowledged, individually, as open problems. But they are a selection — the ones most frequently cited, most publicly debated. A more systematic approach asks a harder question: compile every confirmed observational property of gravity, and score Newton and General Relativity against each one. Not against predictions they were designed to make. Against the full set of things gravity demonstrably does.

Twenty-three properties were compiled from direct observational evidence — from gradiometer measurements, from Gravity Probe B, from Vera Rubin's rotation curves, from Juno's magnetic field maps of Jupiter, from JWST's earliest disc observations, from the Pound-Rebka experiment, from Hulse-Taylor. No theoretical extrapolations. Only what instruments have recorded. The scoring standard was strict: credit for a correct mathematical result, no credit for a correct answer through a wrong mechanism, no credit for successor frameworks the original theory's author could not have known.

The Seven Properties No Theory Has Touched

Seven properties scored zero from both Newton and GR. This is not a coincidence of difficult problems deferred. It is a pattern: the seven are precisely the properties that require a physical description of the gravitational field as something — a medium, a substance, an elastic entity with spatial extent and internal structure. Every property that demands physical substance rather than geometric description is unoccupied. Every property that can be answered with an equation on a manifold has been claimed, to varying degrees, by one or both theories. The gap is not random. It has a shape.

Seven confirmed properties — unaddressed by any existing theory

Each of the following is a direct observational finding, not a theoretical inference. None has been framed as a unified gravitational property in the published literature. Together they define exactly what a successor theory must explain.

P7 — Volume phenomenon

Remove a volume of matter — the gravitational response reduces proportionally. The hollow sphere proof. Archimedes' principle. Both demonstrate that gravity couples to density throughout a volume simultaneously — not to a surface or a point. Neither theory treats gravity as volumetric field-atom coupling. Both reduce to point particles or surface treatments.

P9 — Fusion extends the gravitational radius of action

When matter concentrates through nuclear fusion — four hydrogen nuclei combining into one helium nucleus at nuclear separation distances — the same mass-energy occupies a smaller volume. The gravitational field extends further. No existing theory connects nuclear-scale volume unification to macroscopic field extension. The connection has never been stated.

P11 — Apparent inertia as density contrast

Weight is felt not from gravity itself but from resistance to it. In the Dead Sea, the water provides the resistance your muscles normally provide — the field has not changed, the resistance has been externalised. Archimedes' principle is gravity acting on a density contrast throughout two volumes simultaneously, not a surface effect. Neither theory identifies this as the relational phenomenon it is.

P16 — Field sets the envelope

Venus and Earth have the same gravitational envelope. Venus has 92 times Earth's surface pressure and 460°C temperature difference. The gravitational field set the possibility space — chemistry and stellar environment determined which possibility was realised. Neither theory connects gravitational field strength to atmospheric retention, surface temperature, or planetary physical state. The connection has never been made.

P17 — Compression field

The gravitational field is simultaneously the compressor and the container. Disrupting the equilibrium releases stored compression energy explosively — supernovae, Earth's core pressure, the DART impact. No energy is added in the release — containment is removed. Neither theory identifies the field as simultaneous compressor, container, and energy storage mechanism. GR calculates the pressure. It never identifies this physical character of the field.

P22 — Immediate equatorial disc formation

Every rotating gravitational system organises surrounding matter into a flat disc at its equatorial plane — from Saturn's rings to galactic discs, across fourteen orders of magnitude in scale. The critical observation: disc structure appears at the first observable moment of formation, before angular momentum dissipation has had time to operate. Both theories predict the equilibrium geometry. Neither explains why the order is present from the outset.

P23 — Rotation rate and magnetic dipole correlation

Every solar system body with a measured rotation rate and magnetic field shows the same pattern: fast prograde equatorial rotators have strong aligned dipoles; slow, retrograde, or highly inclined rotators have weak, anomalous, or absent fields. Standard models explain each case with a separate giant impact or tidal event. The correlation across all bodies as a unified gravitational pattern has never been stated, framed, or tested in the literature.

The Pattern in the Gap

Newton scores on geometric and kinematic properties exclusively. Every zero is on a property requiring physical substance. GR recovers Newton's zeros on process rates, frame-dragging, and the gravity-EM coupling cluster — genuine advances. But both theories share the same seven blind spots. The shared blind spot defines the shape of what is missing: a physical description of the gravitational field as an entity with spatial extent, elastic character, and the capacity to store and release compression energy. That description has never been attempted. Not because the properties are unknown. Because the question has not been asked.

The Shielding Argument — Why This Is Not Academic

Of the six failures above, the shielding problem deserves to be dwelt on, because it is the one that exposes the gap between description and understanding most viscerally — and the one that carries the most direct consequence for the energy problem this series has been building toward.

Consider what it means to understand a force. When Maxwell described electromagnetism physically — not just with equations, but as a field with a structure, a medium that carried it, a mechanism by which charges interact — the consequences were immediate and technological. Within decades: electric motors, radio, transformers, generators. The field could be manipulated because it was understood as a physical thing. It could be blocked because you could put something between the field and the object. It could be shaped because you understood what was doing the shaping.

Gravity has received no equivalent. We have the equations. We have the predictions. We have confirmed those predictions with extraordinary precision on the 5% of the Universe where they apply. What we do not have is a physical description of what gravity is — what is doing the gravitating, what medium carries the interaction, what mechanism produces the equivalence of gravitational and inertial mass. And in the absence of that description, shielding is not merely difficult. It is not even a well-defined problem. You cannot block something you cannot describe. You cannot engineer something you can only observe.

The Consequence, Stated Plainly

Every civilisation in human history has been governed by a single constraint: we can describe what gravity does, but we cannot modify it, redirect it, attenuate it, or stand outside it. That constraint has not been lifted by 350 years of increasingly sophisticated equations. It will not be lifted by more equations. It will be lifted by the first physical description of what gravity actually is — which is the description physics has never attempted, because the assumption that we already understand it has made the attempt seem unnecessary.

The stars are not mysterious because they are far away. They are mysterious because they are doing something with gravity that we have no physical account of. The energy we cannot replicate is the energy of a process we describe with equations while treating its medium as nothing and its mechanism as a postulate.

Two Smoking Guns — Experiments That Have Not Been Run

The scorecard is not only an inventory of what existing theories miss. It is a map of where to look. Two specific anomalies surfaced during the property audit that are not widely discussed, are testable with existing laboratory equipment, and point directly at the gap between the current framework and a physical account of gravity.

01
Temperature and Weight — The Direction Is Wrong

General Relativity's stress-energy tensor Tμν includes thermal energy as a gravitational source. Its prediction: a heated metal object should weigh more, because thermal energy contributes to the total mass-energy content. Experiments have found the opposite — weight decreases with temperature, consistently, across materials. The experimental finding has been individually noted and individually explained away. It has never been examined systematically across multiple materials and temperatures as a potential falsification of GR's Tμν thermal energy claim.

The test: Systematic weight measurement across multiple materials at controlled temperatures, looking for a consistent direction. If weight decreases with temperature across all materials, this is a direct contradiction of GR's prediction and a direct confirmation that thermal energy does not source gravity in the way Tμν assumes. Testable with a precision balance and a temperature-controlled chamber.
02
Blueshift Asymmetry — A Signature No One Has Looked For

General Relativity predicts perfect symmetry between gravitational redshift (photon climbing a gravity well, losing energy) and blueshift (photon falling into a gravity well, gaining energy). The mathematics is symmetric. A physical elastic medium — a field with genuine physical properties — would not necessarily be symmetric: compression on descent and rarefaction on ascent are not identical processes in a medium that stores energy elastically. The asymmetry, if it exists, would be a direct signature of the medium's physical character. Nobody has looked for it explicitly. The measurements exist — they have not been compared for asymmetry.

The test: Systematic comparison of precision redshift and blueshift measurements using existing spectroscopy data, looking for a statistically significant asymmetry between the two directions. A measured asymmetry cannot be explained by GR's symmetric mathematics and would constitute direct experimental evidence of medium physical properties. The data is already collected. The analysis has not been done.

What Mastery Actually Looks Like

There is a pattern in the history of physics that is worth being precise about, because it distinguishes description from understanding in a way the field has not always been careful to maintain. Kepler described planetary orbits with extraordinary precision in 1609 — ellipses, correct to observation, predictive, useful. He had no physical account of why the orbits were ellipses. Newton provided that account in 1687, and the account was physically richer than the description: it predicted not just planetary orbits but tides, the precession of equinoxes, the trajectory of cannonballs. The description was not wrong. The understanding made it a special case of something deeper.

We are, with gravity, at the Kepler stage. We have the ellipses. We can predict where the planets will be to extraordinary precision. We have extended the description to black holes and gravitational waves and the large-scale structure of the Universe. And we have, in six specific and measurable ways, run out of description — reached the edges of the regime where the equations work and found that they do not work beyond it, without understanding why, and without a physical account of what they are describing even where they do work.

The Newton who takes gravity from description to understanding has not yet arrived. What has arrived, for the first time, is the combination of named failures, accumulated evidence, and economic pressure that makes the question impossible to defer.

The Wrong Model of the Sun — Built From a Particle Gun

The fusion programmes being pursued today — tokamaks, ITER, inertial confinement — share a common ancestor that is rarely acknowledged. They are not modelled on the Sun. They are modelled on transmutation experiments conducted a century ago. Rutherford in 1919, Cockcroft and Walton in 1932 — particle accelerators firing nuclei at targets, observing what happens when projectiles strike stationary matter at high kinetic energy. The model that emerged is: heat matter until particles move fast enough to overcome the electrostatic repulsion between nuclei by brute force, and fusion occurs. It is a bombardment model. It is the physics of a particle gun applied to a plasma.

The Sun does not do this. The Sun does something categorically different, and the difference has been visible in plain sight for as long as we have been looking at it.

The Sun self-organises. Nobody heats it from outside. Nobody fires particles at it. Gravity compresses matter until the conditions for fusion are present — and fusion then occurs continuously, stably, for billions of years, without exploding, without requiring external energy input to maintain the plasma. The process is gentle enough that the highest temperature we have directly measured at the Sun's surface — the photosphere — is approximately 5,700 Kelvin. That is the temperature of a blast furnace. It is less than an electric arc welder. Whatever is happening in the solar interior is happening under gravitational compression at conditions nobody has directly measured, inferred entirely from a stellar model built on the same incomplete gravity physics the scorecard above has audited.

The Spectrum Says Something Different

The hydrogen spectrum from the Sun is an absorption spectrum — dark Fraunhofer lines where hydrogen absorbs specific wavelengths — not an emission spectrum. Hydrogen emitting energy freely would produce bright lines at those frequencies. Hydrogen absorbing energy produces dark ones. The Sun's hydrogen is absorbing. The same absorption pattern appears from every star, from the interstellar medium, from hydrogen clouds across the observable universe. Hydrogen throughout the universe is predominantly in a state of absorbing at its spectral frequencies — not freely emitting into a surrounding plasma. This is what the instruments record. The standard model of stellar fusion — in which hydrogen nuclei fuse and release energy outward as radiation — must account for why the observational signature of that hydrogen is one of absorption, not emission. The accounting is not straightforward.

Then there is the question of what CERN actually does. The LHC accelerates protons to energies at which the proton's internal quark structure becomes directly relevant and collides them head-on. The result is not fusion. It is not transmutation in the classical sense. It is fragmentation — showers of quarks, gluons, and exotic hadrons produced from the collision energy, none of which are stable, none of which persist. At LHC energies, matter is not being combined. It is being unmade. The fact that we use the word "collision" for Rutherford's 1919 experiment and for LHC runs today obscures that these are entirely different physical regimes operating on entirely different principles. Neither of them is what the Sun does. And we have not, in a century of trying, produced what the Sun does — because we have been trying to reproduce a model of the Sun derived from particle guns, not a physical understanding of how gravity organises matter into stable self-sustaining fusion.

The question the standard model does not comfortably answer: if fusion in the solar core is producing the energy output attributed to it, and if that process is essentially a high-energy collision process as the bombardment model implies, why does the Sun not behave like a bomb? The standard answer is pressure equilibrium — the outward radiation pressure of fusion exactly balances the inward gravitational compression. But this equilibrium is posited inside a body whose interior has never been directly observed, using a gravity model that accounts for 53.9% of gravity's confirmed observational properties, and a nuclear reaction rate calculated from cross-sections measured in particle bombardment experiments at energies and densities that do not replicate the solar interior. The equilibrium is assumed, calculated from assumptions, and then used to validate the assumptions. The chain is circular at every link.

What the Sun does that we have never replicated

Self-organises under gravity into stable fusion without external energy input. Sustains the process for billions of years without exploding. Maintains a photosphere at 5,700 K while producing 3.8 × 10²⁶ watts. Presents an absorption hydrogen spectrum, not an emission spectrum. Organises surrounding matter into a flat equatorial disc across eight planets and billions of smaller bodies. Drags its entire gravitational field in rotation with it. Does all of this as a physical entity — not a mathematical solution to a field equation.

—
What every fusion programme on Earth does instead

Heats plasma to 150 million Kelvin — ten times the estimated solar core temperature — to replicate by brute kinetic energy what the Sun achieves by gravitational compression and self-organisation. Requires continuous external energy input to maintain the plasma. Has not achieved net energy output after seventy years and $65 billion. Operates from a specification derived from particle bombardment experiments conducted a century ago — not from a physical understanding of what the Sun is doing.

The discrepancy between the temperature at which Earth fusion programmes operate and the temperature at which the Sun's photosphere sits is not an engineering detail. It is a signal. If the Sun is achieving fusion at effective temperatures dramatically lower than what the bombardment model requires — through gravitational compression, self-organisation, and physical processes that the incomplete gravity model does not account for — then the target that ITER is engineering toward is not the Sun's target. It is the target derived from firing particles at each other in a laboratory and fitting a model to the results. The Sun was not consulted.

And yet the funding flows toward confirming the model, not testing it. And yet ITER continues on the same specification — the same incomplete physical account of what the Sun is doing — with $65 billion committed and a timeline that has been extending for fifty years. And yet every dark matter non-detection is reported as a new constraint on particle properties rather than as evidence that the particle does not exist. And yet the Hubble tension — a 10% disagreement in the fundamental constant of cosmology, confirmed across independent datasets — is framed as a problem to be resolved within the framework rather than a signal that the framework needs revision. And yet the inverse square law has never been tested between a few AU and the Oort Cloud, the exact regime where the rotation-curve anomaly present in every galaxy we have measured might already be detectable in our own solar system, and nobody is looking.

This is not an accusation against individual scientists. Most of the physicists working within the standard framework are working carefully and honestly on the questions the framework poses. The problem is upstream: the questions the framework poses are not the questions that would falsify it. A framework that responds to every anomaly by adding an invisible term — dark matter, dark energy, a cosmological constant — is not a framework that can be falsified. It is a framework that can be extended indefinitely. And a framework that cannot be falsified is not physics. It is accounting.

The inventory above is not a counsel of despair. It is a map. Every item on it is a specific question with a specific shape — and specific shapes admit specific experimental approaches. The Oort Cloud boundary can be tested. The blueshift asymmetry can be tested with existing spectroscopy data. The temperature-weight relationship can be tested with a precision balance and a temperature-controlled chamber. The inverse square law can be tested at intermediate solar distances with existing deep-space missions. None of these requires a new accelerator. None requires a new satellite. Most require a decision to look — to treat the existing anomalies as falsification candidates rather than calibration adjustments.

The clues, read together, point in one direction: toward a physical description of the medium that gravity operates in, and through, and — this series will argue — as. The vacuum is not empty. The Casimir effect is measured. The Higgs field pervades space. Gravitational waves propagate through something. The question is not whether a medium exists. The question is whether physics is willing to ask what it is.

We are not at the end of gravity's story. We are at the end of the chapter where we confused describing it with understanding it. The next chapter has a different question at its centre: not what does gravity do, but what is doing it.
